Separation Of Effects

What the redesign alone can do vs what else must change

This is the core decision split for leadership. C3A should be judged as a first-fold fix first, not as the entire growth program.

What C3A alone can realistically improve

Reduce bounce on mobile. C3A makes price, fit, and trust visible earlier, which directly addresses early exits.

Lift ATC rate. It helps shoppers qualify themselves faster and removes Royal-driven decision noise in fold 1.

Improve purchase CVR modestly. A realistic redesign-only modeled range is 0.59–0.94%.

What must happen beyond the redesign

Later-funnel clarity must improve. Size selection, proof, financing interpretation, and purchase reassurance still matter after the first fold.

Traffic quality must improve. Referral-heavy traffic and weak organic mix can cap CVR even if the page gets better.

The 1.8% target needs a full stack. Redesign + experiment wins + traffic cleanup + post-click funnel improvements are required to make that number plausible.

CEO Recommendation

Position C3A as the most credible mobile-first direction. It won with both buyer pairs and most directly addresses the current first-fold failure.
Use bounce and ATC as immediate proof points. Those are the metrics most directly supported by the research.
Treat 1.8% purchase CVR as the business target, not as a guaranteed forecast.
Do not scale paid until the redesign wins on ATC. The current page still underperforms on orient, qualify, and commit.
Fix traffic quality in parallel with CRO. With 63% referral, 0.2% organic, and mixed bounce signals, acquisition quality is part of the performance story.
Validate purchase CVR over a longer window. Keep the 14-day post-rollout purchase check exactly as planned, after ATC proves the redesign is working.
Recommended experiment frame: Control vs C3A · 50/50 split · ATC primary · ~4,200 visitors per arm · purchase CVR validated 14 days post-rollout.
